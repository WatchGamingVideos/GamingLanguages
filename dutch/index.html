Dutch is a West Germanic language, a subdivision of the Germanic languages that also includes English, German, Afrikaans, Frisian and Yiddish. (Danish, Swedish and Norwegian are also Germanic languages, but belong to a different branch, the North Germanic languages.)

Dutch is an official language in the Netherlands and Belgium. The Dutch-speaking part of Belgium is known as Flanders, so some people refer to the language spoken there as Flemish. However, in spite of some differences in pronunciation and vocabulary, the Flemish and the Dutch speak the same language. They use the same spelling rules, dictionaries and grammars, and they have set up a joint organisation called the Nederlandse Taalunie (Dutch Language Union). The term “Flemish” is best used to refer to the dialects spoken in Flanders or to language features that only occur in Flanders but not in the Netherlands.

Learning Dutch Online

  • FutureLearn: Introduction to Dutch: a free online course that teaches the basics of Dutch in three weeks. The course content was created by the University of Groningen in the Netherlands. The next “session” starts on 14 November.
  • FutureLearn: Introduction to Frisian: a free online course that teaches the basics of Dutch in three weeks. The course content was created by the University of Groningen in the Netherlands. The next “session” starts on 26 September.
    Frisian is closely related to Dutch but is considered as a language in its own right.
  • Niederländisch lernen im Internet: eine Übersicht von hilfreichen Sites: overview of online resources for people who speak German.
  • Niederländisch lernen online: a course with 100 lessons (30 of which are free of charge) with MP3 files spoken by native speakers of Dutch. The course is accompanied by a book.
  • Babbel.com: Online Niederländisch lernen.
  • Learn Dutch with DutchPod101.com (on YouTube) and its associated website provide resources to learn Dutch (as spoken in the Netherlands).
  • Learn Dutch (on YouTube) and its associated website by Bart De Pau also provide resources to learn Dutch (as spoken in the Netherlands).
  • Niederländisch Nachhilfe Online: commercial tutoring and support for native speakers of German who are learning Dutch, especially at secondary school.
  • TAALBLAD.BE is an electronic magazine in Dutch for people who are learning Dutch as a foreign language. The website's content is available under a Creative Commons licence.
  • Oefen hier je Nederlands: a website where non-native speakers can practice their Dutch language skills.
  • NEDBOX: Oefen je Nederlands online: a website where non-native speakers can practice their Dutch language skills. It is not a Dutch language course. (The website was developed by the KULeuven and several other partners.)
  • Huis van het Nederlands (Brussel): De Taalgarage - adviespunt voor zelfstudie: contact point for questions relating to learning Dutch through self-learning. The page Taalgarage - selectie materialen lists several types of resources to learn Dutch on your own: course books, grammars, vocabulary books, pronunciation guides, spelling guides, and books about the usage of Dutch in a professional context.

Other Links

  • VlaamseTaal.be: website by Filip Camerman about Flemish, i.e. the variant of Dutch spoken in Flanders. The site focuses on linguistic developments in Flanders that differentiate “Flemish” from Dutch as it is spoken in the Netherlands.
  • Nederlandse Taalunie: Nederlands internationaal: webpage about support for students, lecturers and educational instutions that teach or promote the Dutch language outside the Dutch language area.
