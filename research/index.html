Self-Regulated Learning

  • Oxford, Rebecca L.: Teaching & Researching: Language Learning Strategies. Routledge, 2010. ISBN 9780582381292.
    This book is heavily based on research. It presents the Strategy Self-Regulation (S2R) Model of language learning, including practical applications of the model.

Language Learning Strategies

  • Oxford, Rebecca L.: Language Learning Strategies: What Every Teacher Should Know. Heinle, 1990. ISBN 978-0-8384-2862-7. (342 pages)
    This book is primarily for teachers of foreign languages at secondary, university and adult levels. It is mainly about teaching (language) learning strategies but the survey of language learning strategies may also be useful for learners.
  • Griffiths, Carol: The Strategy Factor in Successful Language Learning. Multilingual Matters, 2013. (240 pages) ISBN 9781847699404.
  • Hurd, Stella; Lewis, Tim, eds.: Language Learning Strategies in Independent Settings. Multilingual Matters, 2008. (352 pages) ISBN 9781847690975.
    This book is mainly based on research on language learning at the secondary level.
  • Cohen, Andrew D.: Strategies in Learning and Using a Second Language. 2nd edition. Routledge, 2010. (440 pages) ISBN 9781408253991.
    Another book aimed at language teachers that is based on research, for example, on strategies to learn Spanish grammar.

Theories and Research on Language Acquisition

How Languages are Learned

Lightbown, Patsy M.; Spada, Nina: How Languages are Learned. Oxford Handbooks For Language Teachers. 4th Edition. Oxford University Press, 2013. (272 pages) ISBN 9780194541268.
This book discusses theories of both first and second language acquisition. It is aimed at language teachers, not researchers, and does not require a background in psycholinguistics, so it is also accessible to general readers with an interest in language learning theories. Nevertheless, the focus is on theories; the book does not provide lesson plans or materials that teachers can directly use in the classroom.
See also the three-part interview with Patsy Lightbown and Nina Spada on YouTube: part 1, part 2, part 3.

Effective Language Learning

Graham, Suzanne: Effective Language Learning: Positive Strategies for Advanced Level Language Learning. Modern Languages in Practice. Clevedon: Multilingual Matters, 1997. ISBN 1-85399-379-6.
This book investigates difficulties that learners of French and German experience when they transition to an advanced level, and the strategies and affective processes that help them overcome these difficulties.
