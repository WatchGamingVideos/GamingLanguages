ETJ English

ETJ English is the YouTube channel of Elliott, a native speaker originating from Bristol. His videos covers various aspects, such as pronunciation, grammar and vocabulary.

  • English Lesson: Shortening Words (part 1) (ETJ English on YouTube, 4 minutes, 18.04.2016).
    This video is about forms such as I'd (I would) and we'd (we would). It presents several examples.
  • How to Start Conversations With "Small Talk" (ETJ English on YouTube, 5 minutes, 19.04.2016).
    This video covers the following phrases:
    1. How are you? How's it going? (I'm very well, thank you. How are you?)
    2. What do you do? (Possibly followed up with "How's business?")
    3. What did you think aobut [something in the news]?
    4. Have you ever been to [your country]? / What countries have you travelled to?
    5. What do you do outside of work?
  • English Speaking Lesson: Even Though / If / When (ETJ English on YouTube, 4 minutes, 25.04.2016).
    This video is about the usage of "even though", "even if" and "even when". "Even when": e.g. "Even when I go to work, I bring food with me." "Even if:" e.g.: "Even if I sleep for eight hours, I'm still tired the next day." (This person usually sleeps for less than eight hours.) "Even though": e.g. "Even though I went shopping for the whole day, I still couldn't find the suit I was looking for."
    (This video can also be used for advanced shadowing.) This video was made private or deleted some time after 2019.
  • How To Use "Much" and "Many" | English Lesson (ETJ English on YouTube, 4 minutes, 26.04.2016).
    This video explains the difference between countable and non-countable nouns and the usage of "much" and "many".
  • How To Speak English Fluently (ETJ English on YouTube, 5 minutes, 28.04.2016).
    The "secret" is thinking in English. When you are alone, you can also think out loud. He also recommends translating everyday conversions (that you have had) into English.
  • English Pronunciation Lesson: 'V' and 'W' sounds (ETJ English on YouTube, 6 minutes, 29.04.2016).
  • How To Improve Your English Speaking Skills (ETJ English on YouTube, 4 minutes, 03.05.2016).
    1. Practise speaking with a native speaker. You can easily find a teacher online.
    2. Mirror native speakers. You can listen to, for example, a YouTube video and record your own voice while mimicking the speaker. You can then listen to the recording and compare your recording to the video, see where you have problems and repeat the process.
    3. Learn a new phrase or a new sentence every day.

    (This video can also be used for advanced shadowing.)
  • English Phrases: "Difficult" Explained (ETJ English on YouTube, 4 minutes, 04.05.2016).
    (This video can also be used for advanced shadowing.)
  • How To Improve Your English Vocabulary (ETJ English on YouTube, 6 minutes, 06.05.2016).
  • Present Continuous: Will, Going to and Gonna (ETJ English on YouTube, 4 minutes, 09.05.2016).
    (This video can also be used for advanced shadowing. The blog post mentioned in the video is no longer available online.)
  • Too Busy To Learn English? (ETJ English on YouTube, 8 minutes, 10.05.2016).
    This video makes suggestions such as (1) using podcasts, (2) listening to English music, (3) talking to yourself (or singing in the shower), (4) using English learning apps (such as Cambly, Duolingo, Telegram). This video was made private or deleted some time after 2019.
  • How to learn English Alone (ETJ English on YouTube, 6 minutes, 14.05.2016).
  • Learn English For FREE (ETJ English on YouTube, 7 minutes, 16.05.2016).
    1. Use YouTube: there are many English language teachers on YouTube, so it is a good idea to subscribe to their channels. In addition, there are many vlogs. Watching vlogs is a good way to pick up natural English.
    2. Social media (Snapchat, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, etcetera): follow English teacher who post information about the English language.
    3. Blogs: there are many blogs on learning English.
    4. Podcasts are great if you just want to listen and pick up the way [how] native speakers speak.
    5. Talk to native speakers of English.
  • Become a Fluent English Speaker With ETJ English (ETJ English on YouTube, 2 minutes, 17.05.2016).
    Intro video for the YouTube channel, describing which aspects of English Elliott can help you improve.
  • The Best Way To Improve English Speaking: Make Mistakes! (ETJ English on YouTube, 5 minutes, 19.05.2016).
    The Tree Incident. The same incident is also told in the video "I'm So Clumsy!" (05.04.2017). (This is the first video in which Elliott is standing instead of sitting on a couch.)
  • Will Reading Improve My English Speaking? (ETJ English on YouTube, 6 minutes, 23.05.2016).
    (This video can also be used for advanced shadowing.)
  • Phrasal Verbs With Look (ETJ English on YouTube, 11 minutes, 25.05.2016).
    Look up, look up to, look down on, look over ("Can you look over this for me?"), look through (to inspect), look in on, look after, look into (to investigate), look out, look out for, look around.
  • How To Understand Native English Speakers (ETJ English on YouTube, 6 minutes, 26.05.2016).
  • English Listening Practice: My Weekend (ETJ English on YouTube, 7 minutes, 31.05.2016).
  • How To Stay Motivated To Learn English (ETJ English on YouTube, 4 minutes, 02.06.2016).
    Elliott starts this video with a quote from Eric Thomas: “Look, don’t cry to give up! Cry to keep going!!” Don’t cry to quit! When you study hard and you get sick of trying because you don't see any improvement, this can make you feel sad. You need to learn to use your frustration and/or anger as a source of motivation. When someone laughs at you or tells you that you can't do something, go home and study to prove them wrong.
    (This video can also be used for advanced shadowing.)
  • How Many Words Do I Need To Know To Be Good At English (ETJ English on YouTube, 5 minutes, 06.06.2016).
    It is impossible to determine the number of words you need to know to be a good English speaker. You should not exclusively focus on vocabulary or try to learn every word just because you want to. If you want to become a fluent speaker of English, you should try using the words you already know.
    (This video can also be used for advanced shadowing.)
  • What To Do When You're Bored Of Learning English (ETJ English on YouTube, 6 minutes, 07.06.2016).
  • Streak Of Bad Luck: English Listening Practice (ETJ English on YouTube, 5 minutes, 20.06.2016).

  • English Idiom: Tipping It Down (ETJ English on YouTube, 4 minutes, 22.06.2016).
  • English Phrases: Jet Lag / Tiredness (ETJ English on YouTube, 2 minutes, 01.07.2016).
    This is the first of his videos made in Thailand. Elliott explains the terms jet lag, exhausted and drained.
  • Travelling in Thailand: English Listening Practice (ETJ English on YouTube, 3 minutes, 08.07.2016).

  • English Speaking Lesson: Restaurant Phrases (ETJ English on YouTube, 6 minutes, 28.07.2019).
    Covers booking a table, asking for the menu, ordering food, talking about food allergies or vegetarian dishes, ordering the food and paying the bill.
  • Learning English By Setting Goals (ETJ English on YouTube, 4 minutes, 04.08.2016).
    (This video can also be used for advanced shadowing.)
  • How Fast Do Native English Speakers Talk? (ETJ English on YouTube, 6 minutes, 13.08.2016).
    Speed can vary a lot for various reasons, such as the topic one is talking about or one's accent. When learners don't understand native speakers, the main reason is not speed but a lack of practice in listening skills. (This video can also be used for advanced shadowing; it is spoken a bit slower than most of the other videos on this channel.)

    Before you start or continue learning English, you need to decide which variety of English you want to learn, otherwise you will be learning a mixture of accents and terms, and sound confusing to native speakers.

    This is the live lesson that had been announced a few days earlier.
  • How To Stop Being Shy - Learning English (ETJ English on YouTube, 7 minutes, 10.10.2016).
    Elliott has a few recommendations. Of course, you need to continue learning until you feel confident with your English. If you feel nervous about speaking, the best thing you can do is practise speaking to yourself or to somebody you feel close with. You can talk to yourself in the mirror or you can record your own voice and listen back to it. You can also try to overcome your shyness by pretending to be someone else, for example, pretend that you are a native speaker. Do something that scares you every day. The next time you do that same thing, you won't feel so bad. You can also try having just a short conversation with someone, or send a short video or recording to an online teacher.
  • 9 Very British Words & How to Pronounce Them (ETJ English on YouTube, 9 minutes, 16.10.2020).
    The words discussed in the video are the following: bloody, scrap, row, cuppa, snog, naff, knickers, fluke and chuffed.
  • Different Ways to Say "I Don't Know" + British Pronunciation (ETJ English on YouTube, 8 minutes, 05.12.2012).
    Elliott discusses (mostly informal) alternatives such as “(I) Dunno”, “I'm not (too) sure”, “Not a clue” (“I don't have a clue”), “Don't ask me!”, “Who knows?”, “God knows”, “To be honest mate, your guess is as good as mine”, “No idea”, “Don't take my word for it, but …” and “I'm not 100%”. He also points out that the intonation can change the meaning of some of these phrases.
