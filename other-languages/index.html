Rare and Endangered Languages

General Information

  • What is the definition of “rare language”?: question on Linguistics Stack Exchange.
  • Outside of Modern Hebrew, do any previously dead languages have native speakers again?: question on Linguistics Stack Exchange.
  • Tribalingual: organisation founded in 2016 that tries to find speakers of rare languages who can create a (basic) language course for their language, so other people can learn it online.
  • UNESCO: FAQ on Endangered Languages.
  • Language Revival: Securing the Future of Endangered Languages: a self-paced MOOC on edX
  • Mercator: Teaching Endangered Languages.
  • Myles: 5 Reasons to Learn an Endangered Language.

How to Learn Rare Languages

  • Nagel, Donovan: How To Learn Minority and Endangered Languages With Little or No Resources, June 2015.
  • Gust, Jessica: Why and how to learn minority languages, Omniglot (no date).
  • How to learn a minority language - & how not to: a discussion on the Politics.ie forum.

  • Wilhelmsen, Britta: Save an Endangered Language: Learn Cherokee (Tsalagi Gawonihisdi), 30.11.2015.
  • Intensive Maya Language Immersion.

Arabic

  • Learn Arabic with ArabicPod101.com (YouTube) and ArabicPod101.com.
  • Arabe gratuit: French website with free resources for learning Arabic.
  • Learn Arabic with Maha: YouTube channel with playlists covering, among other things, start learning Arabic, essential Arabic phrases, conversational Arabic and Arabic beginner lessons (more than 100 videos).
  • Arabic with Ahamd Salah is an Italian YouTube channel with Arabic language lessons.

Basque

  • Curso Deusto de Euskara: free online course in three levels; uses Spanish as a starting point.
  • Coyos, Jean-Baptiste; Salaberria, Jasone: Le basque pour les nuls. 2009 ISBN 978-2-7540-1239-3.
    Short guide to the Basque language.
  • Beaumont, Jean-Charles: Le Basque unifié (initiation). Assimil. ISBN 9782700501919.
    Introductory course with 50 lessons in the format of a printed book (392 pages) and four audio CDs. The course claims to take you to CEFR level B1.
  • Beaumont, Jean-Charles: Iniciación al Euskara. Assimil. ISBN 9782700520385.
    Introductory course with 70 lessons in the format of a printed book (384 pages) and three audio CDs. The course claims to take you to CEFR level B1.
  • de Rijk, Rudolf P. G.: Standard Basque: A Progressive Grammar. MIT Press, 2007 (1408 pages, posthumous publication). ISBN 9780262042420.
  • Sareko Euskal Gramatika: online grammar of Basque. For an introduction to this grammar resources, see the YouTube video Grammar of Basque (UPV/EHU) (11 minutes, March 2011).
  • Lexilogos: Dictionnaire basque / Basque dictionary.
  • Buber's Baque Page.
  • Euskaltzaindia / Royal Academy of the Basque Language.

Hebrew

  • Learn Hebrew with HebrewPod101.com (on YouTube) and HebrewPod101.com.
  • Ulpan-Or (on YouTube) and ulpanor.com.
  • Chaviva Shalev: Learn Ivrit: YouTube playlist with 14 videos, last updated in June 2013.
  • Miha Ela: Hebräisch lernen: YouTube playlist with 40 videos, last updated in October 2014.
  • eTeacherHebrew and eTeacherHebrew on YouTube provide courses in Hebrew, Biblical Hebrew, Aramaic Hebrew and Yiddish.
  • eTeacherBiblical and the YouTube channel learnClassicalHebrew provide language lessons in Biblical Hebrew, the language used in the Old and New Testament. The courses have been created in cooperation with the Hebrew University of Jerusalem.
  • Hebrew Podcasts / Learn to Speak Hebrew and the YouTube channel Learn to Speak Hebrew provide self-study lessons in contemporary Hebrew.
  • Skyvrit Academy- Learning Hebrew Online: and its YouTube channel Skyvrit Academy provide (commercial) Hebrew language lessons.
  • Zemach-Tendler, Shalamit: Lehrbuch der neuhebräischen Sprache (Iwrit). Buske, 2014. (296 pages) ISBN 978-3-87548-117-4.
  • Kowalik, Sabine; Kramer, Johannes: Einführung in die hebräische Schrift. Buske,
    • 2005. (67 pages) ISBN 978-3-87548-416-8.
